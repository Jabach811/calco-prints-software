CalCo Prints · The journey of one print

It starts with
a single strand.

Every figure we make begins as one long piece of plastic string. This is the trip it takes — from a plain spool to a character on your shelf. Follow the line.

Scroll to unwind
Point A · The spool

One kilometer of plastic, wound up tight.

Filament. It's the raw stuff — a long noodle of PLA that doesn't look like much. No personality. No face. Not yet. Every buddy, keychain and habitat in the shop starts exactly here.

MATERIAL PLAWIDTH 1.75 MMPERSONALITY 0% (SO FAR)
Step 01 · The idea

Then somebody has an idea.

A doodle in a school notebook. A pet that deserves a statue. An inside joke that needs to be a keychain. Ideas come in weird, and weird is the good stuff — we model it on screen until it looks right from every angle.

Step 02 · The print

The printer gets to work.

The strand feeds into a nozzle hotter than your oven and comes out thinner than spaghetti. Layer stacks on layer — hundreds of them — with robot precision. It's basically a hot glue gun with a brain.

NOZZLE 210°CLAYERS 0.2 MMPATIENCE HOURS
Step 03 · The check

Inspected by the CEO.

Every piece gets picked up, turned over and judged — hard. Stringy bits get trimmed, wobbly layers get reprinted. If it's not cool enough to gift, it doesn't ship. The CEO is ten. He has time, and he has standards.

Step 04 · The pack

Packed like it matters.

No sad zip-lock bags. Each figure ships with its name, its series and a little card, so opening it feels like the gift it is — not like leftovers.

Point B · Your shelf

The strand made it.

Now it has a face, a name and a job — living on your desk, hanging off your backpack, judging your homework.

And the line it traveled ends where every CalCo print ends…
